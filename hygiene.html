Health & Hygiene

Some basic practices necessary for maintaining good personal hygiene

Maintaining Proper Hygiene

Ensure that you wash your hands with soap after going to the toilet and before and after meals. Where soap is not available hands can be washed with ash and water. You must take a bath and brush your teeth everyday. If showering isn't possible, a wash all over the body with a wet sponge would do. Make sure you change into clean clothes regularly. Hanging clothes in the sun kills some disease causing germs. To avoid contracting infections, make sure you cover your mouth and nose when sneezing or coughing.

Keeping drinking water safe

Ensure your drinking water is safe by getting your water tested at regular intervals by trained people. A field testing kit is with the Panchayats and they know who these trained people are. Unhygienic handling of water during transportation or while storing within your home can contaminate previously safe water. Know how to ensure safe drinking water.

Routine Immunization

Children who are immunized are protected from diseases, which can often lead to disability or death. All children have the right to this protection. Every girl and boy needs to be fully immunized. Early protection is critical. The immunizations in the child's first year and into the second year are especially important. It is also essential that pregnant women are immunized against tetanus to protect themselves as well as their newborns.

Use of Oral Rehydration Solution and Zinc

Deaths by diarrhoea can be prevented by a simple and inexpensive public health solution - oral rehydration salts therapy (ORS) in combination with zinc formulated as dispersible tablets. Children with diarrhoea should be given lots of fluids and foods to help reduce the severity of the illness. Menstrual Health and Hygiene

Know that unhygienic menstrual practices can cause reproductive tract infections among adolescent girls and women. One should follow hygienic practices during menstruation. Not doing so can result in fungal infections. Repeated infections lead to serious reproductive tract infections and can cause infertility in the long term. Ensure using clean and dry feminine hygiene products such as cloth or sanitary napkins and change them regularly during menstruation. If cloth is used, it is important that it is regularly washed with soap and water, and then dried completely under the sun, before use, as damp clothing can carry germs.
